# Public Claim Boundary Audit

## Purpose

This document audits the public claims of the MB-X.01 / L.O.N. ecosystem.

Its purpose is to verify that public positioning remains inside the central boundary:

```text
measurement != inference != decision
```

The audit exists to prevent a predictable failure mode:

```text
structural measurement being misread as semantic truth
```

This ecosystem must not be presented as a truth oracle, semantic judge, safety certificate, cryptographic proof engine, or final decision system.

## Audit rule

Every public claim must satisfy this rule:

```text
A repository may claim structural observation, detection, measurement, boundary signaling, orchestration, reproducibility, traceability, or falsification.

A repository may not claim final semantic truth, final safety, final correctness, final security, final deployment approval, or final decision authority.
```

Short form:

```text
structural evidence != final truth
measurement != inference != decision
```

---

## Global boundary

The global boundary is:

```text
measurement != inference != decision
decision remains external
```

This means:

```text
measurement = structural signal
inference   = external interpretation
decision    = external action
```

No repository in the public chain should collapse these layers.

## Minimal reviewer checklist

A reviewer should ask:

```text
1. Is the boundary explicit?
2. Is measurement separated from inference?
3. Is inference separated from decision?
4. Are semantic claims avoided?
5. Are safety claims avoided?
6. Are cryptographic proof claims avoided?
7. Are failure cases documented?
8. Are executable results reproducible?
9. Is final decision external?
```

If the answer is yes, the public claim remains bounded.
